GOALS

1. Enter market with unique features.
2. Follow the strict timeline.
3. Create a clean, engaging platform.

PROBLEM

Many people found some storage services to be confusing and hard to navigate. Another issue I found among cloud service users was that they were being slowed down and finding themselves disorganized with the need to use so many different applications to complete tasks or projects.

SOLUTION

Olympus Cloud Storage Service provides one platform that allows users to upload their media one time to edit, save, and send to their clients in a professional presentation. Olympus has more organizational features than any competing service on the market.

Phase One

Discover the user and existing products.

USER
INSIGHTS

Why are cloud services being utilized and are users getting everything they need?

In order to establish holes in the growing cloud storage market, I conducted a user survey to gauge who, what, and why people were using this type of service. I also was looking for any opportunities they were consciously aware of or frustrations they have with what is currently offered. This shed some light on potential holes in the market that I could target.

COMPETITIVE
ANALYSIS

Listen and observe those who already use cloud services.

Because we were entering a highly concentrated market where products are already performing well, analyzing them was imperative. This allowed us to see what competing services were already doing right, wrong, and where they can improve.

Strengths

Box and Dropbox both allow for plugin features if the standard service is not enough. A strength of iCloud is its integration within products. This increases users by default if they own Apple products.

Weaknesses

A weakness of Dropbox is that they do not provide many upgrade features from one level to the next, taking away motivation to choose a more expensive tier. I found the Box's business interface had a slower upload time than competition, pushing some companies away. Apple has optimized iCloud for Apple users, narrowing their market.

Opportunity

Dropbox has a music and media feature that is very basic and not a selling point. To widen their customer base, they can refine these features. Box has a less than ideal syncing system. If they integrate block-level syncing, it will make collaborating and syncing more efficient and therefore more competitive with Dropbox. Apple doesn't have any features that allow you to utilize tools, just simply store. With the ability to edit and collaborate on files, they would level themselves with the competition.

USER
STORIES

Solve existing problems and provide a one-of-a-kind cloud service.

Olympus Cloud is a service in a market that is already saturated. User stories became a big contributor in designing a very unique product. We had to reduce problems users already face and provide an option that would also take them away from what they already know and use.

USER
FLOWS

Find the shortest and simplest way to complete user goals.

For a service that we were trying to minimize confusion and user steps, tasks flow were iterated on more than once. Part of the problem being solved for was congested cloud services, making users less likely to return.

DESIGN

Integrate research into the design solution and strategy.

BRAND
IDENTITY

Start with mindmapping exercises to let the ideas flow.

The Olympus Cloud evolved from Greek Mythology and represented a sense of security, being the home to Greek Gods and Goddesses. The brand icon consists of a cloud and the mountain resembling the product and name.

WIREFRAMES

Wireframes are blueprints for moving into high fidelity designs and user testing.

They were drawn out in a low fidelity format before turning it into a digital mockup to flush out ideas. The focus having the fewest amount of screens and actions to complete tasks with a familiar interface.

Before moving on to high fidelity, I had to clean up varying call-to-action buttons that were inconsistent throughout as well as confusing.

DESIGN
SYSTEM

The collection of brand and design components that provides a consistent standard.

To create a design system, brand identity had to be developed. I began with a stylescape which is a way to display texture, typography, elements, colors, and photos. The standards across the components were then put together to be utilized in designing and developing.

HIGH-FIDELITY
MOCKUP

Adhere to best practices while creating a beautiful and delightful UI.

In order to maintain consistency, I referred back to the stylescape and design system.

The final product aimed to easily create new lists or categories with a familiar UI elements. The Navbar includes an ‘Add’ button which opens the option of list or category indicated by icons. When creating a new category or list, you are prompted within the pop-up with organizing tools such as selecting a color.

The store homepage is meant to portray a tack board or bulletin board where announcements are made. This is where any important information relating to your designated grocery store will be found. From this store homepage, you can search for items and check the stock.

Within a list, you can start the routed trip from the menu option.
