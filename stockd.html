ABOUT

The desire to find a simple and minimal solution to the many pain points customers are having in their weekly shopping trips was the motivation behind this project. Stock’d Grocery Aide is a simple service that streamlines shopping for the customers as well as allowing employees to be more efficient at work.

PROBLEM

Starting before even entering the store, some problems included overcrowded parking lots and forgetting shopping lists at home. Once in the store, parents are concerned with the safety of their kids when taking their eyes off of them, getting distracted by their kids and forgetting items, and frustrations dealing with upset children. Other shoppers were conflicted with store layouts being different every place they went and spending so much time circling around trying to find all the items. Store employees also thought there could be a more efficient way for them to take care of job responsibilities without being stopped by lost shoppers.

SOLUTION

Create a list application with more organization tools than ever before. An application that has a feeling of community without being overwhelming and distracting and one that takes the thinking out of shopping, that way the user can make the most out of their time, keep their kids safe, and spend more time on what really matters instead of that third trip circling the grocery store. This application indirectly makes the jobs of employees more efficient by cutting down the times they are stopped by lost customers or checking stock for them.

DISCOVER

The broad, early phase used to research the problem space.

USER
SURVEY

Gather insights into shopper habits and pain points.

It included questions such as how familiar they are with their frequented grocery store and frustrations they have with shopping trips. At the time of this survey, COVID-19 regulations had been instated requiring social distancing and masks. Some of these frustrations came up such as people not following guidelines.

PERSONAS

FILL OUT WITH SOMETHING

Personas were developed from the user surveys to represent varying profiles of customers using this application. They were referenced throughout the design process to make sure their needs and concerns were being addressed.

COMPETITIVE
ANALYSIS

Discover competing products and the strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities.

Being that there wasn’t any product quite like Stock’d, it took a bit more digging to find other list-making applications that could provide insight. By researching these already-existing examples, I was able to find where Stock'd could capitalize for users.

DEFINE

Synthesize user research to define the problem and overall goals.

USER
STORIES

Prioritize user goals and tasks that need to be present in the product design.

The user stories were created to solve the most impending issues found from the research as well as important tasks that are needed to work through an application. This step helped to keep the design straightforward without unnecessary features.

USER
FLOWS

Put user goals into a visual format step-by-step.

These pathways were the blueprint for the application and frequently referenced in wireframing iterations. My goal at this step was to make them as short and concise as possible in order to optimize the experience.

DOODLES

First step in the ideation process used to encourage free thinking and find creative direction.

It allowed me to put thoughts down on paper without an agenda. I began writing down anything that came to mind about grocery stores and the entire experience. After using the mind-mapping, I was able to doodle them into logo form.

DESIGN

Integrate research into the design solution and strategy.

WIREFRAMES

Wireframes are blueprints for moving into high fidelity designs and user testing.

They were drawn out in a low fidelity format before turning it into a digital mockup to flush out ideas. The focus having the fewest amount of screens and actions to complete tasks with a familiar interface.

Before moving on to high fidelity, I had to clean up varying call-to-action buttons that were inconsistent throughout as well as confusing.

DESIGN
SYSTEM

The collection of brand and design components that provides a consistent standard.

To create a design system, brand identity had to be developed. I began with a stylescape which is a way to display texture, typography, elements, colors, and photos. The standards across the components were then put together to be utilized in designing and developing.

HIGH-FIDELITY
MOCKUP

Adhere to best practices while creating a beautiful and delightful UI.

In order to maintain consistency, I referred back to the stylescape and design system.

The final product aimed to easily create new lists or categories with a familiar UI elements. The Navbar includes an ‘Add’ button which opens the option of list or category indicated by icons. When creating a new category or list, you are prompted within the pop-up with organizing tools such as selecting a color.

The store homepage is meant to portray a tack board or bulletin board where announcements are made. This is where any important information relating to your designated grocery store will be found. From this store homepage, you can search for items and check the stock.

Within a list, you can start the routed trip from the menu option.

Upon clicking the 'add' button, organization options pop up. These include new list or new category. Immediate organization is brought to the dashboard.
Among features like color coding items, calculated total, and amount of items, you can access your designated store's stock of items.
You can access a community within this application. Your chosen home store has it's own page with daily and weekly deals. Add items directly to lists from here!
Open any list that has been made and access a routed experience of the list.
At multiple different access points in the app, you can access and change your home store.
The routed list interface allows you to cross items off as you go. This unique feature is particularly useful when visiting a new store.
